Acceptance of Terms

  1. The EHR system is exclusively for authorized healthcare personnel involved in direct patient care and administrative duties.
  2. Employees must only access patient records relevant to their assigned tasks. Any unauthorized access or use is strictly prohibited.
  3. Each user is responsible for maintaining the confidentiality of their login credentials. Sharing passwords or allowing others to use your account is not permitted.
  4. Any attempt to gain unauthorized access, manipulate data, or bypass security controls is a serious violation that may result in disciplinary action.

CONFIDENTIALITY AND SECURITY

  1. Patient health information is confidential and protected by institutional policies and legal regulations. Employees must handle all data responsibly.
  2. Patient data must not be accessed, shared, or discussed with unauthorized individuals, including colleagues not directly involved in the patient's care.
  3. Employees must log out of the system when leaving their workstations and ensure that screens displaying patient information are not visible to unauthorized persons.
  4. Any suspected security breach or unauthorized access must be reported to the IT or Compliance Department immediately.

PROHIBITED ACTIVITIES

  1. Accessing patient records out of curiosity or personal interest is strictly prohibited.
  2. Using patient information for personal gain, external research, or any unauthorized purpose is not allowed.
  3. Employees must not install unauthorized software or attempt to alter system configurations without proper authorization.
  4. Sharing patient information through unauthorized communication channels (e.g., personal email, messaging apps) is a violation of policy.

ACCURACY AND DOCUMENTATION

  1. All entries in the EHR must be accurate, complete, and recorded in a timely manner.
  2. Falsification or manipulation of data is strictly prohibited. Corrections must follow established protocols.
  3. Employees must verify data entries before entering data to minimize errors that could impact patient care.
  4. Unauthorized changes, deletions, or corrections to patient records must be reported and are subject to investigation.

ACKNOWLEDGEMENT AND ACCEPTANCE

  1. By accessing the EHR system, employees acknowledge that they have read, understood, and agreed to comply with these terms and conditions.
  2. Employees are responsible for staying informed about changes while using the system and will be held accountable for the consequences of any violations.
